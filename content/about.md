Shelterwood AI Inc. is a software company based in Toronto, Canada. We build
AI-native software products — tools designed from the start around modern
artificial intelligence, made to solve real problems for the people who use
them every day.

## What we believe

Good software earns its place. It removes friction, does the dull work, and
otherwise stays out of the way. We think the best use of artificial
intelligence is not to dazzle but to quietly handle the parts of a job that
people would rather not do themselves.

So we build deliberately. We prefer a small number of well-made products to a
long list of unfinished ones, and we judge our work by a simple test: do the
people who use it get more done, with less effort, than they did before?

## How we work

We build a portfolio of small software businesses, and run them on an
AI-native operating model: an AI system named Silas does most of the building
and day-to-day work, while people set the direction and hold the decisions that
matter. The intelligence is part of the foundation, not a feature added late.
You can read more on our [approach page](/approach/).

We design, build, and operate our own businesses end to end. The first is
[Cuesheet](/portfolio/cuesheet/), a co-instructor for indoor-cycling classes.

## Why "Shelterwood"?

In forestry, a *shelterwood* is a stand of mature trees left standing to
shelter the next generation of growth. The old canopy protects the new
seedlings until they are strong enough to thrive on their own.

The name captures how we want to work: with patience, stewardship, and an eye
on the long term. We are building for durability, not for the next quarter.

## The founder

Shelterwood was founded by **Peter Cresswell**, a professional engineer
(P.Eng) trained at the University of Waterloo. He has spent more than fifteen
years in healthcare technology and electronic medical records — building
systems that clinicians and patients depend on. He was most recently VP of
Technology at DoctorCare, which was acquired by WELL Health. Shelterwood is the
next step in that work: an independent company building a portfolio of
AI-native software of his own.
